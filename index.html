Mapping the mission.

Early days

At the start of our project, we focussed on planning and strategising the end goal and purpose of our TTS system. We explored several ideas, considering how best to utilise the technology to serve a meaningful purpose. Our initial concepts included:

  • A learning tool for students
  • A language learning tool
  • An audiobook service
  • A storytelling app

As we progressed, we decided to focus on the learning tool for students after conducting research for our literature review. We came across an informative source that detailed how TTS models have been used by students to engage more thoroughly with content, helping them absorb information better by listening rather than mindlessly reading. This concept particularly intrigued our team, as we are all students, and therefore we are essentially the stakeholders in this project, making it easier to design and develop a tool that directly meets our needs and addresses challenges we face in our own learning.

When we first learnt about the project, our team was enthusiastic to produce a TTS model incorporating the voice of Jim Parsons - the actor renowned for his role of Sheldon Cooper in the Big Bang Theory - a result of our mutual love for the show. To suggest our passion more thoroughly, we named our model C.O.O.P.E.R - standing for the Completely Original and Outstandingly Practical Emotive Robot.

Our Literature Review

As we were finalising our project idea and deciding how to implement our TTS model, our team wrote our literature review. We explored a range of TTS models and software, examined their implementations, traced the development and origins of TTS technology, and considered the ethical implications of integrating generative AI. Our research was thorough and we considered the validity of our sources, only including credible ones in our end product. We're proud to have written a well-researched and thought out literature review that helped us gain some insight and knowledge in how we ourselves can start developing our project and the considerations we would have to make in terms of ethics and ability. If you’re interested, you can read it here!

TTS Development Process

After completing the literature review, our team began the physical process of working on our text-to-speech model, whilst incorporating Sheldon Cooper’s voice into the process. Due to the complex and arduous nature of this project, our mentors recommended that we use a pre-coded model of the AI, and work upon it further to achieve our ultimate goal: produce emotionally synthetic audio from our model.


Following the second meeting with our mentors, we were presented with a variety of GitHub sources that would allow us to proceed with our project. Our options included Kokoro TTS, and the f5 TTS. After work with the former, we discovered that, despite being able to sufficiently utilise Cooper’s voice, and produce good quality audio for our project, it lacked the emotional element that our project required. (include some evidence of the old TTS and the audio that it produced).


Thus, we concluded that it would be best to enhance the latter: the f5 TTS model. We downloaded the file onto VSCode, and began development from there. This was a testing process - we encountered many difficulties throughout. This included the actual running process of the AI - a task that was made all the more lengthy with our lack of GPU, which forced us to run it on the CPU and made processing considerably more difficult. There was also the issue of the machine learning process - stripping down suitable audio files and feeding it to the AI for it to be able to adopt the voice of Sheldon Cooper.


Despite this, our team persevered.

Evaluation

TTS Development process


Working on this project as a team proved to be a very rewarding process, regardless of the challenges that we may have faced along the way. We were able to, not only produce a project that lived up to the standards we were set, but include the Cooper element, which reflected our own personal interests amongst this academic project.


However, this implementation of Cooper, despite being gratifying to us, means that we may not be able to proceed as far with this project as we would like to, due to ethical considerations. We were unfortunately unable to gain clear consent for using Jim Parsons’ voice within our AI system. As we are not currently planning to commercialise this project and release it to the public, this usage does not have any unethical implications, but to broaden the project into a public domain would mean that we would face such issues.
